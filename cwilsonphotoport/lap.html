When taking landscape photos, if you have the ability to try and use a tripod, and a remote shutter release. Also known as a camera remote, it allows for you to take the picture without touch the camera. Because when taking landscapes, you tend to have much lower shutter speeds, which can add blurryness, using a tripod removes the handholding aspect, and the whole remote, allows for the slight bump from the button to not affect your image. However this was taken while handholding my camera, and still got this great picture, so it doesn't always affect the end result to not have that stuff.

First notice about this, it was taken in a wildlife park, behind a closed fence, and I was close to the bear which was behind a sturdy safe fence. Do not get this close to bears, or any animals in the wildlife if you can help it, as they are wild animals, not pets, and will attack if threatened. I was only this close because this bear had been too socialized with humans, and knew that generally unless in specific situations meant no harm, so that's why I could get that close.
